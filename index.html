How This Works

The animals will be grouped together by which habitat they live in, such as the arctic animals go together. Some animals go by themselves or are mentioned twice, which means they are either endangered or are special.

Arctic Animals

The Arctic Head North to enjoy an ice-cold adventure! The Arctic is located at the top of the Earth, and a big part of it is a frozen ocean. So as you go to the Arctic, careful not to stomp too hard, just in case your standing over the ocean! The frozen ocean area temperature goes down to about -40 degrees celsius, which makes it hard for animals to find food, as no plants can grow there. All the animals inhabiting the Arctic has developed to cope with the harsh conditions.


The Animals


The Polar Bear

Even though it's spring in the Arctic, the temperatures only 0 degrees celsius. Polar bear mums have thick fur which keeps them warm when it's freezing. Their cubs are born in a den dug out of the snow, and when they're old enough, they walk on the Arctic ice.

Mini-Facts

Type: Mammal
Food: Seals, small whales, and bird's eggs
Sound: Growl or chuff
Baby Name: Cub
Group Name: A celebration of polar bears

Wild Fact: Polar bears can stand as tall as #.5 metres tall when standing on their hind legs!


Reindeer

When winter arrives in the Arctic, reindeer join together into a group called herds. They travel south to find food and warmer weather than that of the harsh Arctic environment. Sometimes, half a million join the walk! When a group of animals travels long distances, it's called a migration. Reindeer migrations are some of the biggest migrations.

Mini-Facts

Type: Mammal
Food: Lichens, moss or grass
Sound: Bark or whistle
Baby Name: Calf
Group Name: A herd

Wild Fact: Some reindeers walk 4 328 kilometres in one year!


The Puffin

Puffins gather on cliffs and spend most of their time at sea. In the spring, they lay their eggs in burrows. Puffins love to fish in the chilly water of the Arctic. They swim by flapping, as they are flying in the sea.

Mini-Facts

Type: Bird
Food: Mostly fish
Sound: Purr, grunt or groan
Baby Name: Puffling
Group Name: A circus


Wild Fact: A puffin's beak is spiky, so it can catch slippery fish. Also, a puffin's beak turns grey in winter.


Water Mammals

Want to take a chilly dip? Go to the Arctic Ocean. The Arctic is surrounded by water, and its middle is ice. In the winter, the ice gets larger, but in the summer, it starts melting. Some mammals spend their whole lives in the Arctic Ocean, so they have bodies prepared for the cold.

Water Mammal Facts

Polar bears can swim for weeks to find a new home.
Beluga whales have a thick layer of fat, called blubber, to keep them warm.
The long-tusked walrus spends some time in the water, some time on land.
The killing whale, or an orca, is actually a dolphin that can hold it's breath for an amazing 11 minutes.
The harpy seal pup starts to swim when it's just 6 weeks old.



Wolves

When you think you're alone in the woods, you suddenly hear a piercing howl nearby. An Arctic wolf is near. Their white coats are hard to spot in the snow, which helps the wolf hunt with ease. The wolves hunt in packs and they howl to let every other wolf know where it is. As the ground is too hard to dig a burrow, they live in rocky caves.

Mini-Facts

Type: Mammal
Food: Musk ox, reindeer, hares or birds
Sound: Howls, bark, whimper or snarl
Baby Name: Cub
Group Name: A pack

Wild Fact: A wolf howl can be heard from 8 kilometres away!
